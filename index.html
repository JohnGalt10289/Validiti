"The Problem..."

Security provided by complexity

Complicating something mathmatically to make it more difficult for computers to solve is a game that will eventually be lost. Additionally this increase in complexity would slow computers proccessing and data transmission speeds as well as cosume unnecessarily greater amounts of electricity. Using costly methods like radioactive isotope decay to supply a growing market with pseudorandom number sets is logically inefficient as well.

Ideal Solution

What does a perfect solution look like?

In our minds the perfect solution would consist of an ever available global network of random variables you can draw from to supply a global network of needs. Oh yeah and they need to be balanced in supply and in demand, or at the least it should be balancable.
